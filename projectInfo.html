Project Summary

HYPO2 is a high altitude training camp for Olympic athletes. While most people watch the Olympics every 2 years, Olympic athletes are training every second before their events began. HYPO2 is looking for a web-based application to help smooth out their process. This includes taking teams of athletes and having a better scheduling process for these athletes to make it easier for them to get here and get ready to train.

Problems Solutions

Training Schedule

The way schedules are made right now are very time consuming with much back and forth with the coach and company. We, as a team, want to give an option to the coaches so that they can build and modify their schedule without too much communication needed.

Request Services

There is no way to know whether it is okay for an athlete to get a service without verbal communication with the coach. The team goal is to have a notification sent to a coach when an athlete requests a service and once the coach has given permission the appointment will be scheduled.

Hierarchy Access

HYPO2 deals with sensitive medical information from coaches and Olympic athletes that come from all around the world. This project must follow HIPAA regulations. The team has a goal to achieve different account access levels to keep their information private.

In-House Management

One of the ideal outcomes for the project is for this to be an in-house product that is used by all employees. This should be used for tasks like scheduling and signing up teams to come to HYPO2. The goal of this concept is to make communication with teams more accessible.

Technologies

Here is a overview on are Technologies we be integrating into the HYPO2 web portal.

AWS will be used for our hosting services and has features that we will use. These features include: Amazon incognito for logging in users and Amazon SNS API to send email and text to users when needed.

MYSQL will be used for holding the data for different types of users such as: teams, coaches, athletes, employees of HYPO2.

Node JS will be used as our backend framework to interact with our frontend framework and pulling and pushing data to our database.

React will be our frontend framework due to the ease of use and familiarity that many members of the team have with it. React also has a support with Bootstrap to give us a way to make an easy and aesthetic UI.
