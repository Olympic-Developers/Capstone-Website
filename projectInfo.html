Project Summary

HYPO2 is a high altitude training camp for Olympic athletes. While most people watch the Olympics every 2 years, Olympic athletes are training every second before their events began. HYPO2 is looking for a web-based application to help smooth out their process. This includes taking teams of athletes and having a better scheduling process for these athletes to make it easier for them to get here and get ready to train. Challenges of their current process is that most of HYPO2 is ran by hand which means clients of HYPO2 need to email for every need they have. This includes creating a camp, requesting a service, cancelling a service, and so much more.

Envisioned Solution

This is what the team imagines as a solutions. As of right now, most of HYPO2 workflows require constant communication for the easiest task. We hope to make a web portal that gets rid of all this constant communication by taking care of creating the training camps without Gmail, keeping track of camp information in a database rather than in a Google Drive and, finally, allowing clients to request or cancel an in-house and external services directly in the application rather than needing to email HYPO2.

Requirements

The Olympic Developers met weekly with our client, Sean Anthony He is the founder and CEO of HYPO2 sports. We met weekly in order to gather a better idea of the client's vision for the product. For the requirements we had three types of users that will use the application: HYPO2 clients, admin and staff. Due to this, we broke the requirements up into four sections: shared user functionalities, Account-Specific functionalities, Non-Functional, and environmental requirements. Below is a quick overview of the requirements for each section of the project. For more detail visit the Document tab and go to Requirements Document.

Shared User Functionalities

Shared functionalities are functionalities that will be shared between the 3 types of users using the web application. These functionalities will be user accounts, user validation, and notification preferences.

Account-Specific Functionalities

Account-Specific functionalities are functionalities that will be unique between the 3 types of users using the web application. These functionalities will include customer, administrator, and staff member functionalities.

Non-Functional Requirements

Non-Functional Requirements are how we will be testing the Web application. We will be testing the usability of the product. We will have four groups testing throughout the coding process: developers, HYPO2 clients, administrator, and staff.

Environmental Requirements

Environmental requirements are requirements that were made mandatory by our client. These requirements are a transferable repository, desktop compatibility, user manual, As-built report, and appearance.

Technologies

Here is a overview on the technologies that we will be integrating into the HYPO2 web portal. For more information on the technologies, check out our tech feasibility in the documents tab.

AWS will be used for our hosting services and has features that we will use. These features include: Amazon incognito for logging in users and Amazon SNS API to send email and text to users when needed.

MYSQL will be used for holding the data for different types of users such as: teams, coaches, athletes, employees of HYPO2.

Node JS will be used as our backend framework to interact with our frontend framework and pulling and pushing data to our database.

React will be our frontend framework due to the ease of use and familiarity that many members of the team have with it. React also has a support with Bootstrap to give us a way to make an easy and aesthetic UI.
